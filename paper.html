Academic Paper

Understanding the needs, coping styles and well-being of teenage mothers

Authors:
Sally I. Maximo a
Patricia Pablo b
Vera Joy Panganiban b
Rhodette Grace Saguid b
Jovy Mae Padsuyan b
Sheela Mae Rafael b

Published in: Saint Louis University Research Journal, Volume 41, No.2 (2010)


Abstract:

This determined the prevalent needs, coping styles, and levels of well-being among teenage mothers. It also looked into the relationship of coping with the different dimensions of well-being. Results show that the survival and ego needs among these mothers are less satisfied compared to their social needs. The prevalence of survival needs supported the study's hypothesis. Problem-focused coping is dominantly used than active emotion-focused coping, while avoidant emotion-focused coping is least utilized. This did not confirm the hypothesis that emotion-focused coping will be more utilized. Both problem-focused and active emotion-focused coping is inversely related. This strengthened the hypothesis that effective coping contributes to well-being. The findings have implications on increasing support for these teenagers who are facing challenges that are unique to their situation as young parents.
